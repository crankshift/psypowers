# assessment-scales

Standardised assessment scales are the backbone of measurement-based care. This skill covers scoring, interpretation, cutoffs, and administration guidance for the most commonly used psychometric instruments in mental health settings.

| Scale | Abbreviation | Measures | Items | Range | Time (min) | Recommended frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Patient Health Questionnaire-9 | PHQ-9 | Depression severity | 9 | 0–27 | 2–5 | Every 2–4 weeks |
| Beck Depression Inventory-II | BDI-II | Depression severity | 21 | 0–63 | 5–10 | Every 2–4 weeks |
| Generalized Anxiety Disorder-7 | GAD-7 | Anxiety severity | 7 | 0–21 | 2–3 | Every 2–4 weeks |

## Depression scales

### PHQ-9 (Patient Health Questionnaire-9)

**Description:** Self-report measure based on DSM criteria for major depressive disorder. Each of 9 items rated 0 (not at all) to 3 (nearly every day) over the past 2 weeks.

**Scoring and cutoffs:**

| Score range | Severity | Clinical action |
|---|---|---|
| 0–4 | Minimal / none | No treatment indicated |
| 5–9 | Mild | Watchful waiting; repeat at follow-up |
| 10–14 | Moderate | Treatment plan indicated (therapy, consider medication) |
| 15–19 | Moderately severe | Active treatment (therapy + medication consideration) |
| 20–27 | Severe | Immediate treatment; medication + therapy; consider referral |

**Reliable Change Index (RCI):** Approximately 6 points. A change of >=6 points between administrations is statistically reliable (not attributable to measurement error).

**Clinical significance threshold:** Crossing below 10 (moving from clinical to non-clinical range).

**Item 9 — Suicidality screen:** "Thoughts that you would be better off dead, or of hurting yourself in some way." ANY non-zero response (>=1) requires follow-up assessment. This is a screen, not a risk assessment — a score of 0 does not rule out suicidality; a score of 1+ requires structured follow-up (see C-SSRS section).

**Special considerations:**
- Validated across diverse populations including primary care, psychiatric, medical, and community settings.
- Available in 80+ languages.
- Free to use (no copyright restrictions — developed by Pfizer, placed in public domain).
- Sensitivity 88%, specificity 88% for major depression at cutoff >=10.
- Can be used as both screening and severity monitoring tool.
- Not validated for children under 12 (use PHQ-A for adolescents).

### BDI-II (Beck Depression Inventory-II)

**Description:** 21-item self-report scale. Each item has 4 statements (0–3) reflecting increasing severity. Covers affective, cognitive, and somatic symptoms of depression. Time frame: past 2 weeks.

**Scoring and cutoffs:**

| Score range | Severity |
|---|---|
| 0–13 | Minimal |
| 14–19 | Mild |
| 20–28 | Moderate |
| 29–63 | Severe |

**Reliable Change Index (RCI):** Approximately 8 points.

**Clinical significance threshold:** Crossing below 14.

**Special considerations:**
- Copyright held by Pearson — requires purchase for clinical use.
- More comprehensive than PHQ-9 (21 vs 9 items) but takes longer.
- Includes items on sadness, pessimism, past failure, loss of pleasure, guilt, punishment, self-dislike, self-criticalness, suicidal thoughts (item 9), crying, agitation, loss of interest, indecisiveness, worthlessness, energy loss, sleep changes, irritability, appetite changes, concentration difficulty, tiredness, loss of interest in sex.
- Item 9 (suicidal thoughts/wishes) parallels PHQ-9 item 9 — same follow-up protocol required.
- Validated ages 13–80.
- Strong psychometric properties (internal consistency alpha typically .90+).

## Anxiety scales

### GAD-7 (Generalized Anxiety Disorder-7)

**Description:** 7-item self-report measure. Each item rated 0 (not at all) to 3 (nearly every day) over the past 2 weeks. Although designed for GAD, it is a good screener for anxiety disorders generally.

**Scoring and cutoffs:**

| Score range | Severity | Clinical action |
|---|---|---|
| 0–4 | Minimal | Monitor |
| 5–9 | Mild | Watchful waiting |
| 10–14 | Moderate | Treatment plan indicated |
| 15–21 | Severe | Active treatment |

**Reliable Change Index (RCI):** Approximately 4 points.

**Clinical significance threshold:** Crossing below 10.

**Special considerations:**
- Free to use (public domain).
- Sensitivity 89%, specificity 82% for GAD at cutoff >=10.
- Also screens reasonably well for panic disorder, social anxiety disorder, and PTSD (though disorder-specific measures are preferred for those).
- Available in 60+ languages.
- Not specific to GAD — elevated scores warrant differential assessment for which anxiety disorder is primary.

### BAI (Beck Anxiety Inventory)

**Description:** 21-item self-report scale. Each item describes a common anxiety symptom rated 0 (not at all) to 3 (severely) over the past week. Emphasises somatic/physiological symptoms of anxiety.

**Scoring and cutoffs:**

| Score range | Severity |
|---|---|
| 0–7 | Minimal |
| 8–15 | Mild |
| 16–25 | Moderate |
| 26–63 | Severe |

**Special considerations:**
- Copyright held by Pearson — requires purchase.
- Strong somatic focus means it may be less sensitive to cognitive/worry-based anxiety (GAD-7 may be better for that).
- May over-detect anxiety in medical populations with genuine somatic conditions.
- Good discriminant validity — better at distinguishing anxiety from depression than some other measures.

## PTSD scale

### PCL-5 (PTSD Checklist for DSM-5)

**Description:** 20-item self-report measure. Each item rated 0 (not at all) to 4 (extremely) over the past month. Maps onto DSM-5/ICD-11 PTSD symptom clusters.

**Scoring and cutoffs:**

| Score range | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| 0–30 | Below threshold |
| 31–33 | Probable PTSD (clinical cutoff) |
| 34–80 | Above clinical threshold |

**Recommended provisional diagnostic cutoff:** 31–33 (research varies; 33 is commonly used in VA/DoD settings, 31 in civilian research).

**Cluster scoring:**

| Cluster | Items | DSM-5 criterion | Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| B — Intrusion | 1–5 | Criterion B | Re-experiencing: memories, dreams, flashbacks, emotional distress, physical reactions to reminders |
| C — Avoidance | 6–7 | Criterion C | Avoidance of internal reminders, avoidance of external reminders |
| D — Cognition/mood | 8–14 | Criterion D | Negative beliefs, blame, negative emotions, loss of interest, detachment, inability to experience positive emotions, amnesia |
| E — Arousal/reactivity | 15–20 | Criterion E | Irritability, reckless behaviour, hypervigilance, exaggerated startle, concentration problems, sleep disturbance |

**DSM-5 diagnostic rule:** At least 1 B item, 1 C item, 2 D items, and 2 E items rated >=2 (moderately).

**Reliable Change Index (RCI):** Approximately 10 points.

**Clinical significance threshold:** Crossing below 33.

**Special considerations:**
- Free to use (developed by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs).
- Criterion A (trauma exposure) must be established separately — PCL-5 measures symptom severity only.
- Available with or without Criterion A assessment and Life Events Checklist (LEC-5).
- Can be used with a past-month or past-week time frame (past-month is standard).

## General wellbeing

### WHO-5 (WHO Well-Being Index)

**Description:** 5-item positive measure of wellbeing. Each item rated 0 (at no time) to 5 (all of the time) over the past 2 weeks.

**Scoring:**
- Raw score: sum of 5 items (range 0–25).
- Percentage score: raw score multiplied by 4 (range 0–100).

**Cutoffs:**

| Raw score | Percentage | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| >=13 | >=52 | Adequate wellbeing |
| <13 | <52 | Poor wellbeing — further assessment indicated |
| <10 | <40 | Screen positive for depression — administer PHQ-9 or BDI-II |

**Special considerations:**
- Free to use (WHO, public domain).
- Positively worded — less face-threatening than symptom-focused measures; good for routine monitoring.
- Validated as depression screener (sensitivity ~86% at cutoff <13).
- Very brief (<2 min) — suitable for every-session administration.
- Available in 30+ languages.
- Does not measure specific psychopathology — use as a general barometer, not diagnostic tool.

## Substance use scales

### AUDIT (Alcohol Use Disorders Identification Test)

**Description:** 10-item screening tool developed by WHO. Items cover consumption (1–3), dependence symptoms (4–6), and harm/consequences (7–10). Mixed scoring: items 1–8 scored 0–4, items 9–10 scored 0, 2, or 4.

**Scoring and cutoffs:**

| Score range | Risk level | Recommended intervention |
|---|---|---|
| 0–7 | Low risk | Alcohol education |
| 8–15 | Hazardous use | Brief intervention (FRAMES) |
| 16–19 | Harmful use | Brief intervention + continued monitoring |
| 20–40 | Possible dependence | Referral for specialist assessment |

**Zone-based intervention:**

| Zone | Score | Action |
|---|---|---|
| I | 0–7 | Prevention / education |
| II | 8–15 | Simple advice |
| III | 16–19 | Simple advice + brief counselling + continued monitoring |
| IV | 20–40 | Referral to specialist |

**Special considerations:**
- Free to use (WHO, public domain).
- AUDIT-C (first 3 items only) can be used as a quick screen: cutoff >=4 for men, >=3 for women.
- Validated globally across diverse populations.
- Scores may need cultural context — drinking norms vary significantly.

### CAGE Questionnaire

**Description:** 4-item screening tool. Yes/No responses to:
- **C** — Have you ever felt you should **Cut** down on your drinking?
- **A** — Have people **Annoyed** you by criticizing your drinking?
- **G** — Have you ever felt bad or **Guilty** about your drinking?
- **E** — Have you ever had a drink first thing in the morning (**Eye-opener**)?

**Scoring and cutoffs:**

| Score | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| 0–1 | Low probability of alcohol problem |
| >=2 | Clinically significant — further assessment warranted |

**Special considerations:**
- Very brief — useful as a quick screen in primary care or intake.
- Less sensitive than AUDIT — better for detecting dependence than hazardous use.
- Does not quantify drinking — use AUDIT or timeline followback for consumption assessment.
- Free to use.

## Somatic symptom scale

### PHQ-15 (Patient Health Questionnaire-15)

**Description:** 15-item self-report measure of somatic symptom severity. Each item rated 0 (not bothered at all) to 2 (bothered a lot) over the past 4 weeks.

**Scoring and cutoffs:**

| Score range | Severity |
|---|---|
| 0–4 | Minimal |
| 5–9 | Low |
| 10–14 | Medium |
| 15–30 | High |

**Special considerations:**
- Free to use (public domain).
- Covers: stomach pain, back pain, limb pain, headaches, chest pain, dizziness, fainting, palpitations, shortness of breath, constipation/diarrhoea, nausea/gas/indigestion, fatigue, sleep problems, menstrual cramps (score 0 for male patients on this item), sexual problems.
- Useful for tracking somatic presentations alongside depression/anxiety measures.
- Somatic symptoms are common in depression and anxiety — scores should be interpreted alongside PHQ-9/GAD-7.
- High PHQ-15 with low PHQ-9/GAD-7 may suggest bodily distress disorder (ICD-11 6C20).

## Session feedback scales

### ORS (Outcome Rating Scale)

**Description:** 4-item ultra-brief measure of client functioning. Four visual analog scales (10 cm each) covering: (1) Individual/personal wellbeing, (2) Interpersonal/close relationships, (3) Social/work/school, (4) Overall sense of wellbeing.

**Scoring:**
- Each scale: 0–10 (measured in cm from left anchor).
- Total: 0–40.

**Cutoffs:**

| Score | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| <25 | Below clinical cutoff — indicates distress/impairment (clinical range) |
| >=25 | Above clinical cutoff — functional range |

**Reliable Change Index (RCI):** Approximately 5 points.

**Special considerations:**
- Part of the Partners for Change Outcome Management System (PCOMS) / FIT (Feedback-Informed Treatment).
- Administered at the START of every session — tracks between-session change.
- License required for clinical use (some versions freely available for training/research).
- Expected trajectory: scores should increase over treatment. Failure to show early improvement (by session 3–4) predicts poor outcome.
- Child version (CORS) available for ages 6–12.

### SRS (Session Rating Scale)

**Description:** 4-item ultra-brief alliance measure. Four visual analog scales (10 cm each) covering: (1) Relationship (feeling heard, understood, respected), (2) Goals and topics (working on what client wants), (3) Approach or method (good fit for client), (4) Overall (session felt right).

**Scoring:**
- Each scale: 0–10.
- Total: 0–40.

**Cutoffs:**

| Score | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| <36 | Below cutoff — alliance concern; explore with client |
| >=36 | Adequate alliance |

**Special considerations:**
- Administered at the END of every session — measures within-session alliance.
- Scores tend to cluster high (ceiling effect). What matters more is whether there is a DROP from the client's baseline — even a score of 34 may be significant if the client usually scores 39.
- Low SRS scores are the therapist's cue to have an open conversation about the alliance. Do not interpret defensively.
- Part of PCOMS/FIT alongside ORS.
- License required for clinical use.

## Suicidality assessment

### C-SSRS (Columbia-Suicide Severity Rating Scale)

**Description:** Structured interview (not a self-report questionnaire). Assesses suicidal ideation and behaviour through a branching question structure.

**Suicidal ideation section (5 questions with increasing severity):**

| Question | Level | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Wish to be dead | Wish to be dead or not alive, wish to fall asleep and not wake up |
| 2 | Non-specific active suicidal thoughts | General suicidal thoughts ("I want to kill myself") without method, intent, or plan |
| 3 | Active suicidal ideation with any methods (not plan) without intent to act | Thinking of methods but no specific plan and no intent |
| 4 | Active suicidal ideation with some intent to act, without specific plan | Some intent to act on thoughts |
| 5 | Active suicidal ideation with specific plan and intent | Specific plan AND intent to carry it out |

**Suicidal behaviour section:**
- Actual attempt (with or without injury)
- Interrupted attempt (interrupted by external circumstance)
- Aborted attempt (self-interrupted)
- Preparatory acts or behaviour
- Non-suicidal self-injurious behaviour (differentiated from suicidal behaviour)

**Scoring:** No single numeric score. Classified by:
- Highest ideation severity (1–5)
- Presence/type of behaviour
- Lethality of any attempt
- Timing (lifetime vs. since last visit vs. current)

**Intensity of ideation sub-ratings:** Frequency, duration, controllability, deterrents, reasons for ideation.

**Special considerations:**
- Free to use for clinical, research, and training purposes (Columbia University).
- Multiple versions: full, screener, since-last-visit, lifetime/recent.
- Screener version (6 questions) suitable for non-mental-health settings (ED, primary care).
- "Since last visit" version is optimal for ongoing therapy monitoring.
- Requires training for administration — available free at cssrs.columbia.edu.
- Does not replace clinical judgment about risk — it structures the assessment.
- Any positive response on ideation (>=1) at intake warrants a safety plan discussion.
- Any response at level 4 or 5 requires immediate risk assessment and safety planning.

## When to use which scale

| Clinical question | Primary scale | Secondary/supplementary |
|---|---|---|
| Is this patient depressed? | PHQ-9 | BDI-II |
| How anxious is this patient? | GAD-7 | BAI |
| Does this patient have PTSD? | PCL-5 | — |
| Overall, how is this patient doing? | WHO-5 or ORS | — |
| Is the patient drinking too much? | AUDIT | CAGE (quick screen) |
| Is the patient having somatic symptoms? | PHQ-15 | — |
| How was today's session? | SRS | — |
| Is this patient suicidal? | C-SSRS | PHQ-9 item 9 (screen only) |
| Is treatment working? | ORS (each session) + PHQ-9/GAD-7/PCL-5 (every 2-4 weeks) | — |

## Interpreting change over time

For any repeated measure, always calculate whether change is reliable:

```
Change = Score_current - Score_previous
If |Change| >= RCI → Reliable change (not measurement error)
If direction is improvement AND crosses clinical cutoff → Clinically significant improvement
```

**Quick RCI reference:**

| Scale | RCI (approx.) | Clinical cutoff |
|---|---|---|
| PHQ-9 | 6 | 10 |
| BDI-II | 8 | 14 |
| GAD-7 | 4 | 10 |
| PCL-5 | 10 | 33 |
| ORS | 5 | 25 |

## Common mistakes

- **Using wrong cutoffs.** Each scale has its own validated thresholds. Do not apply PHQ-9 cutoffs to BDI-II or vice versa.
- **Confusing screening with diagnosis.** Scales identify probable cases — they do not diagnose. A PHQ-9 score of 15 does not mean the patient "has moderate-severe depression"; it means the score is in that range and clinical assessment is indicated.
- **Ignoring item 9 on PHQ-9 (or item 9 on BDI-II).** Any non-zero score on the suicidality item requires structured follow-up. This is a patient safety issue, not a scoring detail.
- **Not tracking scores over time.** A single score is a snapshot. Measurement-based care requires repeated administration to detect change, plateau, or deterioration.
- **Using RCI without understanding it.** A patient's PHQ-9 dropping from 18 to 15 (3-point change) is NOT a reliable improvement — it is within measurement error. Only changes >= RCI are meaningful.
- **Administering copyrighted scales without a licence.** PHQ-9, GAD-7, WHO-5, PCL-5, C-SSRS, AUDIT, and CAGE are free. BDI-II, BAI, ORS, and SRS require purchase/licence.
- **Using the BAI as a general anxiety measure.** The BAI has a strong somatic focus and may miss cognitive anxiety. GAD-7 is better for worry-predominant presentations.
- **Forgetting population validity.** Most scales are validated on adult populations. Using adult cutoffs for adolescents or older adults without adjustment may produce inaccurate results. Always check the validation population.
- **Interpreting SRS scores in isolation.** SRS scores tend to cluster high. A score of 35 might look "almost perfect" but is below the clinical cutoff of 36 and may indicate an alliance problem the client is reluctant to voice directly.
